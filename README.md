# Barley Proteome Project

Structured R pipeline for the analysis of large-scale _Hordeum vulgare_ (barley) proteomics datasets across multiple varieties, species, and environmental conditions.

## Overview
This project implements a structured workflow to process, integrate, and analyse quantitative proteomics data from a panel of barley varieties under abiotic stress conditions. The data were generated at the Chair of Plant Systems Biology (Schwechheimer Lab, TUM) and the Chair of Proteomics and Bioanalytics (Kuster Lab, TUM). The computational workflow was developed in collaboration with the Chair of Computational Mass Spectrometry (Wilhelm Lab, TUM), with the aim of enabling reproducible integration and comparison of large-scale proteomics datasets. The project focuses on the analysis of hydrolitic enzymes relevant for the malting industry in collaboration with the Carlsberg Research Group. 

## Features
- Quality control of large-scale proteomics datasets (filtering, consistency checks, summary metrics)  
- Normalisation of protein intensities across samples and conditions  
- Integration of protein abundance data across multiple varieties and species  
- Comparative analysis under different abiotic stress conditions  
- Automated generation of publication-ready visualisations  

## Technical Highlights
- Developed in R 
- Designed for reproducible and modular data analysis  
- Compatible with remote execution on Linux/HPC environments  
- Handles large multi-file datasets typical of proteomics workflows
